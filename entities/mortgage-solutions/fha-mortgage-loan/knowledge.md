# FHA Mortgage Loan

## Executive Summary

An FHA Mortgage Loan is a government-insured home loan designed to make homeownership more accessible for qualified borrowers. FHA loans are especially popular with first-time home buyers because they may allow lower down payments and more flexible credit qualification standards than some conventional loan programs.

Although FHA loans are often associated with first-time buyers, they are available to eligible repeat buyers as well, provided the borrower, property, and loan meet current FHA program requirements.

## Borrower Explanation

An FHA loan can be a helpful option if you want to buy a primary residence but need a mortgage program with flexible qualification guidelines. The Federal Housing Administration does not lend money directly. Instead, FHA insures loans made by approved lenders, which helps make the program available to qualified borrowers.

Affinity Home Lending helps borrowers compare FHA financing with Conventional, VA, USDA, Down Payment Assistance, and other mortgage options so they can make an informed decision.

## Best For

An FHA loan may be a good fit for:

- First-time home buyers
- Buyers with limited down payment savings
- Borrowers with moderate credit histories
- Homebuyers using eligible gift funds
- Buyers purchasing a primary residence
- Borrowers who want to compare flexible financing options

## Benefits

- Lower down payment options for qualified borrowers
- Flexible credit guidelines compared to some conventional options
- Eligible gift funds may be allowed
- Competitive financing options
- Many FHA loans are assumable, subject to approval and guidelines

## Considerations

- FHA mortgage insurance is required in most cases
- FHA loan limits vary by county
- FHA is generally for primary residences
- The property must meet FHA minimum property standards
- Another loan option may be better depending on the borrower’s full profile

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is FHA only for first-time home buyers?

No. FHA financing is available to both first-time and repeat home buyers who meet program requirements.

### Does FHA require mortgage insurance?

Yes. Most FHA loans include both upfront and annual mortgage insurance premiums.

### Can I use gift money with an FHA loan?

Yes. Eligible gift funds may be used for the down payment and certain closing costs, subject to FHA documentation requirements.

### Can FHA be used for investment property?

FHA loans are generally intended for owner-occupied primary residences, not investment properties.
